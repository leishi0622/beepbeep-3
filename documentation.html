Documentation

Tutorial

BeepBeep has been the subject of a tutorial at the 10th International Conference on Runtime Verification (RV 2016), held in September 2016 in Madrid. You can find below the slides for this tutorial.

When RV Meets CEP (RV 2016 Tutorial) from Sylvain Hallé

You can also read the 24-page research paper that accompanies these slides, which was also published at RV 2016:

  • S. Hallé. (2016). When RV Meets CEP. Proc. RV 2016, Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science 10012, 68-91.

Research Papers

BeepBeep has been the subject of extensive academic research. You can find below a number of papers that have been published about BeepBeep in the recent past.

  • S. Hallé, S. Gaboury, B. Bouchard. (2016). Activity Recognition Through Complex Event Processing: First Findings Proc. ATSE 2016.
  • S. Hallé, S. Gaboury, B. Bouchard. (2016). Towards User Activity Recognition Through Energy Usage Analysis And Complex Event Processing Proc. PETRA 2016.
  • S. Hallé, S. Varvaressos. (2014). A Formalization of Complex Event Stream Processing

Made at LIF


BeepBeep was developed through the research done at LIF, the Formal Computer Science Laboratory in Université du Québec à Chicoutimi, Canada.

Interested?

BeepBeep is free software, released under the GNU LGPL. This means you can include it as a library in any project, including closed-source commercial software.
